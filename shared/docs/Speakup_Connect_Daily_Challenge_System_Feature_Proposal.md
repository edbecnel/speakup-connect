# Student Engagement – Daily Challenge System

**Document Status:** Proposed Feature  
**Priority:** Medium (Recommended for Pilot Expansion)  
**Applies To:** Speakup Connect SchoolComm, Speakup Connect Campus

---

# Purpose

The Daily Challenge System is designed to encourage students to open Speakup Connect every day, even when there are no new announcements or messages waiting for them.

Unlike traditional trivia systems that can often be solved instantly using AI tools, the Daily Challenge System focuses on activities that promote participation, creativity, reasoning, observation, and engagement within the school community.

The objective is to make Speakup Connect not only the school's primary communication platform, but also a daily destination for students.

---

# Goals

The Daily Challenge System should:

- Encourage daily app usage.
- Increase student engagement.
- Promote school spirit and community.
- Reward consistent participation.
- Encourage collaboration and friendly competition.
- Provide meaningful non-academic interaction.
- Be enjoyable without distracting from the educational mission of the school.

---

# Design Philosophy

Traditional online trivia has become significantly less effective due to modern AI tools.

Instead, Speakup Connect should emphasize challenges that require one or more of the following:

- Observation
- Creativity
- Logical reasoning
- Local school knowledge
- Participation
- Physical presence on campus

The more context-specific a challenge is, the less likely it can simply be answered by an AI chatbot.

---

# Daily Challenge Framework

Rather than implementing a single "Trivia" feature, Speakup Connect should provide a flexible Daily Challenge framework capable of supporting many different challenge types.

Only one Daily Challenge is active at a time.

Challenge types rotate automatically.

This prevents the experience from becoming repetitive while allowing schools to contribute many different kinds of content.

---

# Supported Challenge Types

## 1. Campus Challenge ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

Students solve clues that require observing or exploring the school campus.

Examples:

- Identify a classroom from a cropped photograph.
- Find a hidden mascot poster.
- Count architectural features.
- Locate a specific landmark.
- Identify a campus location.

These challenges naturally encourage students to become familiar with their campus.

---

## 2. Mystery Photo / Mystery Zoom

Students identify an object shown through:

- Extreme close-up images
- Blurred images
- Cropped photographs
- Slowly revealed images

Students guess the object.

Each incorrect day can reveal a slightly larger or clearer image.

Examples for the extreme close-up image/photograph.:

- Basketball
- Science equipment
- Musical instrument
- Cafeteria tray
- School logo

---

## 3. Spot the Difference

Students compare two similar school-related images and identify the differences.

School events, classrooms, laboratories, sports activities, and campus photographs are all suitable subjects.

Simple, fast, and highly addictive.

---

## 4. Guess the Teacher

Teachers voluntarily provide fun facts or photographs.

Examples:

- Baby picture
- Favorite hobby
- Favorite snack
- Favorite quote
- Favorite vacation destination

Students guess which teacher is being featured.

---

## 5. School History

Questions about the school's history.

Examples:

- Founding year - When was the school founded?
- Former principals
- Historical events
- Campus expansion
- School traditions
- Which building is the oldest?
- Who was the first principal?

These help preserve institutional knowledge and school pride.

Most answers are not easily found online.

---

## 6. Logic Challenge ⭐⭐⭐⭐

Rather than testing memorization, students solve reasoning problems.

Possible formats:

1. Numerical & Grid Logic
	Puzzles in this category rely entirely on numbers, math rules, or grid deduction. They do not create an image when finished.
	- **Sudoku**
	- **Kakuro**
	- **Number Sequences**
	- **KenKen** (or **Calcudoku**): A highly popular grid puzzle that combines Sudoku-style digit placement with basic arithmetic operators (addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division).
2. Visual & Spatial Logic
	These puzzles require you to manipulate shapes, see geometric patterns, or reveal an image using your eyes and spatial reasoning.
	- **Matchstick Puzzles**
	- **Visual Logic** _(such as Nonograms, Slitherlink, or spot-the-difference)
	- **Tangrams**: A classic, universally recognized dissection puzzle consisting of seven flat geometric shapes (tans) that players must arrange to form specific silhouettes without any overlap._
3. Situation & Lateral Thinking
	These are narrative-based logic riddles. They require you to move items or characters step-by-step under strict operational constraints.
	- **River Crossing Puzzles**
	- **Deduction Puzzles** (such as logic grid Einstein riddles or truth-teller/liar paradoxes)
	- **The Knights and Knaves Paradoxes**: Famous logic puzzles populated by characters who either always tell the truth (knights) or always lie (knaves), where you must deduce facts based on their spoken statements.

## 7. Poll of the Day

Students answer fun opinion-based questions.

Examples:

- Favorite school event
- Favorite lunch
- Favorite sport
- Would You Rather...
- Favorite elective subject

Results are revealed after voting closes.

---

## 8. Prediction Challenge

Students predict outcomes before they occur.

Examples:

- Sports competitions
- Weather
- School events
- Academic competitions

Results are announced later.

---

## 9. QR Campus Hunt ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

QR codes are hidden around campus.

Students scan them to earn:

- Points
- Achievement progress
- Secret clues
- Bonus rewards

This encourages students to explore the campus.

---

## 10. Caption Contest

A humorous school photograph is posted.

Students submit captions.

Students vote for their favorite submission.

---

## 11. Emoji Challenge

Students interpret emoji sequences.

Example:

📚 + ☕ + 😴

Possible answer:

Studying late for exams.

---

## 12. Guess the Sound

Students identify short audio clips.

Examples:

- School bell
- Laboratory equipment
- Musical instrument
- Sports whistle
- Cafeteria sounds

---

## 13. Campus Scavenger Hunt

Students locate specific items around campus.

Evidence may include:

- Photograph submission
- QR code scan
- Teacher verification

---

## 14. Finish the Drawing

Students complete an unfinished illustration.

Suitable for art-related activities.

---

## 15. Secret Code Challenge

Students decode encrypted messages.

Example:
KHOOR

Answer:
HELLO

Difficulty increases over time.

---

## 16. Teacher Fun Facts

Teachers submit interesting facts about themselves.

Students identify which teacher matches each clue.

Examples:

Which teacher...

- likes fishing?
- owns chickens?
- has climbed Mt. Apo?
- speaks three languages?

---

## 17. Today in School History

Highlights important historical moments.

Examples:

- School anniversaries
- Championship victories
- Historical campus photographs
- Alumni achievements
- Former principals

---

## 18. Riddle of the Day

Original riddles created by faculty or student organizations specifically for the school.

Custom riddles are much harder to search online.

---

## 19. Mini STEM Challenge

Simple prediction or reasoning activities.

Examples:

- Which bridge design is strongest?
- Which paper airplane flies farther?
- Predict the outcome of a simple experiment.

Science teachers may contribute content.

# Weekly Challenges

Some challenges may span multiple days.

Examples include:

- Escape room puzzles
- Treasure hunts
- Multi-stage mysteries
- Campus detective games
	Example progression:
	1. Decode a message.
	2. Solve a logic puzzle.
	3. Use the answer to unlock another clue.
	4. Find something on campus.
	5. Solve the final mystery.

Students return daily to unlock the next stage.
This encourages students to return throughout the week.

---

# Reward System

Rewards should focus primarily on recognition rather than prizes.

Possible rewards include:

- Daily participation points
- Weekly bonus points
- Consecutive-day streaks
- Experience (XP)
- Achievement badges
- Achievement levels
- Special titles
- Seasonal achievements

Example badges:

- First Challenge Completed
- Seven-Day Streak
- Puzzle Master
- Campus Explorer
- History Buff
- Logic Master
- STEM Explorer
- Community Helper

Rather than focusing solely on prizes, the app should reward participation and consistency.

Students can earn:

- ⭐ Points
- 🔥 Daily streaks
- 🏅 Achievement badges
- 🎖 Experience levels
- 🏆 Leaderboard rankings

Many students value recognition as much as physical prizes.

# Content Management

Challenges should be created through the administrative portal.

Authorized content creators may include:

- School Administrators
- SSLG Officers
- Faculty Advisors
- Club Moderators
- Teachers (optional)

Challenges may be scheduled in advance.

Images, audio, QR codes, and attachments should be supported where appropriate.

---

# Moderation

Student submissions should support moderation before publication.

This includes:

- Caption contests
- Photo submissions
- Drawing contests
- Creative responses

Moderation tools should include:

- Approve
- Reject
- Hide
- Report
- Remove inappropriate content

---

# Notifications

Students may receive optional notifications such as:

- New Daily Challenge available
- Weekly Challenge unlocked
- Challenge ending soon
- Congratulations! You earned a badge.
- Your class has moved into first place.
- Results are now available.

Notification preferences should be configurable by each user.

---

# AI-Resistant Design Principles

Daily Challenges should prioritize activities requiring:

- Observation
- Participation
- Creativity
- Local knowledge
- Physical interaction
- Logical reasoning

Whenever possible, challenge content should be unique to the school community rather than relying on publicly searchable facts.

---

# Accessibility

Daily Challenges should remain accessible to all students.

Where possible:

- Images should include alternative text.
- Audio challenges should include optional captions.
- Color should never be the sole indicator.
- Challenges should be usable with screen readers.
- Difficulty should vary throughout the year to appeal to different age groups.

# Expected Benefits

The Daily Challenge System transforms Speakup Connect from a communication tool into a platform that students actively want to visit every day.

Expected benefits include:

- Increased daily active users.
- Improved awareness of school announcements.
- Greater student participation.
- Stronger school identity.
- Increased interaction between students, teachers, and organizations.
- Improved visibility of clubs and student activities.
- Positive reinforcement through recognition and achievement.
- Long-term student engagement without relying solely on external rewards.
