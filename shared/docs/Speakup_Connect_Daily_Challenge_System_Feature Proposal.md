# Speakup Connect Feature Proposal
# Daily Challenge System

## Overview

One of the best ways to encourage students to open Speakup Connect every day is to provide a fun, interactive feature that changes daily. Rather than relying solely on trivia questions—which can often be answered instantly using AI—the app should offer a variety of challenge types that emphasize observation, creativity, participation, reasoning, and school involvement.

Instead of branding this feature as **Daily Trivia**, a broader and more engaging concept would be:

# Daily Challenge

Each day, students receive one new challenge. The challenge type rotates automatically, keeping the experience fresh and encouraging daily engagement.

---

# Objectives

- Encourage students to open the app every day.
- Increase participation within the school community.
- Promote school pride.
- Reward consistent engagement.
- Create opportunities for friendly competition.
- Minimize the ability to "cheat" using AI.

---

# Possible Daily Challenge Types

## 1. Campus Challenge ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

Students solve clues that require exploring or observing the school campus.

Examples:

- Find this location from a cropped photo.
- Which classroom is shown?
- Count the steps on the main stairway.
- Find the hidden mascot poster.
- Which building has this architectural detail?

These require students to actually be present at school.

---

## 2. Spot the Difference

Two nearly identical school-related photos are shown.

Students identify five differences.

Simple, fast, and highly addictive.

---

## 3. Mystery Zoom

Show an extreme close-up photograph.

Examples:

- Basketball
- Science equipment
- Musical instrument
- Cafeteria tray
- School logo

Students guess the object.

Each incorrect day can reveal a slightly larger image.

---

## 4. Weekly Escape Puzzle ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

A larger challenge spanning the entire week.

Example progression:

1. Decode a message.
2. Solve a logic puzzle.
3. Use the answer to unlock another clue.
4. Find something on campus.
5. Solve the final mystery.

This encourages students to return throughout the week.

---

## 5. Guess the Teacher

Use fun clues such as:

- Baby photo
- Favorite quote
- Favorite food
- Childhood drawing
- Favorite hobby

Students guess which teacher it is.

Teachers would likely enjoy participating.

---

## 6. School History Challenge

Questions about the school's history.

Examples:

- When was the school founded?
- Why was the mascot chosen?
- Which building is the oldest?
- Who was the first principal?

Most answers are not easily found online.

---

## 7. Daily Logic Puzzle ⭐⭐⭐⭐

Rather than trivia, present logic problems.

Examples:

- Number patterns
- Matchstick puzzles
- Sudoku
- Kakuro
- Nonograms
- River crossing puzzles

These reward reasoning instead of memorization.

---

## 8. Poll of the Day

Students vote on fun questions.

Examples:

- Would you rather...
- Favorite school event
- Favorite lunch
- Favorite sport
- Favorite school club

Results are revealed the following day.

---

## 9. Prediction Challenge

Students predict future outcomes.

Examples:

- Which section wins Sports Fest?
- Will it rain tomorrow?
- Which club wins the competition?
- Which team wins today's game?

Results are announced afterward.

---

## 10. Hidden QR Hunt ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

QR codes are hidden around campus.

Scanning them earns:

- Points
- Badges
- Secret clues
- Achievement progress

Encourages exploration and physical activity.

---

## 11. Caption Contest

Post a humorous school photo.

Students submit captions.

Students vote on the funniest submission.

Winning captions earn points.

---

## 12. Emoji Puzzle

Students interpret emoji sequences.

Example:

📚 + 🏃 + 😴

Possible answer:

Studying all night before exams.

---

## 13. Guess the Sound

Play a short audio clip.

Examples:

- School bell
- Cafeteria noise
- Science lab equipment
- PE whistle
- Keyboard typing

Students identify the sound.

---

## 14. School Scavenger Hunt

Students locate specific items around campus.

Examples:

- Red flower
- School motto
- Oldest classroom
- Library mascot

Students submit photos as proof.

---

## 15. Finish the Drawing

Show half of a drawing.

Students complete the other half.

Excellent for art clubs.

---

## 16. Secret Code Challenge

Students decode encrypted messages.

Example:

KHOOR

Answer:

HELLO

Difficulty increases over time.

---

## 17. Teacher Fact Quiz

Interesting facts submitted by teachers.

Examples:

Which teacher...

- likes fishing?
- owns chickens?
- has climbed Mt. Apo?
- speaks three languages?

---

## 18. This Day at School

Historical moments.

Examples:

- School anniversaries
- Former principals
- Championship victories
- Old campus photographs

Promotes school pride.

---

## 19. Riddle of the Day

Original riddles written specifically for the school.

Custom riddles are much harder to search online.

---

## 20. Mini STEM Challenge

Examples:

- Which paper airplane flies farther?
- Which bridge design holds more weight?
- Predict the outcome of a simple experiment.

Science teachers can contribute ideas.

---

# Reward System

Rather than focusing solely on prizes, the app should reward participation and consistency.

Students can earn:

- ⭐ Points
- 🔥 Daily streaks
- 🏅 Achievement badges
- 🎖 Experience levels
- 🏆 Leaderboard rankings

Example achievements:

- 7-Day Streak
- Puzzle Solver
- Campus Explorer
- History Buff
- Logic Master
- Debate Champion
- Community Helper

Many students value recognition as much as physical prizes.

---

# Group Competitions

Leaderboards can also be maintained for:

- Sections
- Grade levels
- Clubs
- Student organizations
- Academic houses (where applicable)

Example:

Grade 10

1. Section A — 8,240 points
2. Section B — 8,120 points
3. Section C — 7,985 points

Friendly competition encourages classmates to participate together.

---

# Designing AI-Resistant Challenges

Because modern AI tools can answer many traditional trivia questions, Daily Challenges should emphasize activities that require:

- Observation
- Creativity
- Participation
- Local school knowledge
- Physical presence
- Logical reasoning

Challenges involving the school's campus, teachers, events, and community are much more difficult for AI to solve instantly.

---

# Recommended Feature

Rather than creating a simple "Trivia" section, Speakup Connect should include a flexible **Daily Challenge** feature.

Challenge types could include:

- 🧩 Logic Puzzle
- 📷 Mystery Photo
- 🏫 Campus Challenge
- 🗳️ Poll of the Day
- 🎵 Guess the Sound
- 🧠 Riddle
- 🎯 Mini Quiz
- 🔍 Scavenger Hunt
- 😂 Caption Contest
- 🎨 Creative Challenge

By rotating challenge types each day, Speakup Connect becomes more than a communication platform—it becomes part of the students' daily routine. This increases engagement, strengthens school community, provides opportunities for student participation, and gives teachers, SSLG officers, and administrators a fun and meaningful way to interact with the student body.
