# Speakup Connect: Secure Onboarding Plan for MONHS Staff and Students

## Purpose

The purpose of this plan is to make sure that Speakup Connect is only used by authorized MONHS staff and students.

Speakup Connect may be publicly available for download from the App Store. However, downloading or installing the app should not automatically allow anyone to access MONHS or any other school’s private system.

Only students approved by the school through the official roster or pilot roster should be able to activate student accounts. Only staff members approved by the school should be given staff, admin, or Org Admin access.

The goal is to protect student privacy, prevent impersonation, prevent unauthorized access, and support MONHS responsibilities under the Data Privacy Act and DepEd child protection policies.

---
## Public App, School-Controlled Access

Speakup Connect may be publicly available for download from the App Store. However, installing the app must not automatically give anyone access to MONHS or to any other school’s private student reporting system.

Speakup Connect is intended to become a multi-school platform. Each school that uses the app should have its own protected school environment, its own approved users, its own student roster, its own staff accounts, and its own privacy and child protection responsibilities.

The app provider can supply the technical safeguards, onboarding tools, role-based permissions, and configuration options. However, each participating school remains responsible for deciding who is authorized to use the system, who may access student reports, how reports are handled, and how the school complies with applicable privacy and child protection requirements.

For MONHS, this means that MONHS controls who may access the MONHS environment. A student must be approved through the MONHS roster-based onboarding process, and staff access must be approved by MONHS.

---
## Initial School Organization and Org Admin Setup

Before students or staff can be onboarded, each school must first have a protected school environment created inside Speakup Connect.

This school environment may also be called the school organization, school account, or school tenant.

A school organization should not be created automatically just because someone downloads the app and types the name of a school.

The first Org Admin for a school should be created only after the school has been verified and the school has approved the person who will serve as the initial Org Admin.

---

## New School Setup Request Process

If someone downloads Speakup Connect and wants to set it up for their school, the app may allow that person to submit a School Setup Request.

Submitting a School Setup Request should not automatically create an active school environment, grant administrator access, allow staff invitations, or allow student onboarding.

The request should begin a verification process. The school environment should only be created after the app provider has verified that the request is legitimate and that the school head, principal, or authorized representative has approved the setup.

Recommended process:

1. The person downloads and opens Speakup Connect.
2. The person selects “Request School Setup” or “Set Up My School.”
3. The app collects basic request information, such as:
   - School name
   - School address or location
   - Requestor’s name
   - Requestor’s role at the school
   - Requestor’s contact information
   - School head or principal contact information
   - Reason for requesting setup
4. The request is submitted as “Pending Review.”
5. The app provider reviews the request.
6. The app provider verifies that the request is legitimate and connected to the actual school.
7. The school head, principal, or authorized representative confirms approval.
8. The school identifies the first Org Admin.
9. The app provider creates the protected school environment.
10. The app provider sends a secure, one-time Org Admin invitation to the approved person.
11. The approved Org Admin activates the school account.
12. Staff and student onboarding can begin only after the Org Admin is active and the school’s onboarding rules are configured.

A School Setup Request is only a request. It does not create a school account, does not grant administrator access, and does not allow access to student or staff features until the school is verified and approved.

---

## In-App Flow for a New School Request

When a new user opens the app, the app may show options such as:

1. Activate Student Account
2. Activate Staff/Admin Account
3. Request School Setup
4. I Need Help

If the user selects “Request School Setup,” the app should collect school and requestor information, then submit the request for review.

The app should display a message such as:

“Your school setup request has been submitted for review. Speakup Connect will verify the request with the school head or authorized representative before creating a school environment or granting administrator access.”

The app should not allow the following unsafe workflow:

- User downloads the app
- User types a school name
- User creates a school account
- User assigns self as Org Admin
- User starts inviting students

Instead, the app should require:

- School setup request
- Provider review
- School verification
- Principal or authorized representative approval
- First Org Admin invitation
- Controlled activation

---

## School Organization Request Process

For MONHS and future participating schools, the recommended process is:

1. The school expresses interest in using Speakup Connect.
2. The app provider collects basic school information.
3. The app provider verifies that the request is legitimate.
4. The school head, principal, or authorized representative identifies the first Org Admin.
5. The app provider creates the protected school environment.
6. The app provider issues a unique, time-limited Org Admin invitation.
7. The approved Org Admin downloads the app.
8. The approved Org Admin activates the account using the secure invitation.
9. The system assigns the Org Admin role only to that approved person.
10. The Org Admin activation is recorded in the audit log.

---

## Verifying the School

Before creating a school organization, the app provider should make reasonable efforts to verify that the request actually came from the school.

Verification may include one or more of the following:

- Written approval from the principal or school head
- Official school letter or authorization
- Memorandum of agreement or pilot approval document
- Confirmation through an official school email address, if available
- Confirmation through known school contact information
- In-person meeting or video meeting with school leadership
- Confirmation from DepEd-recognized school personnel, where appropriate

The purpose is to prevent an outsider, former student, parent, or unauthorized person from creating a fake school environment or claiming to represent a school.

---

## First Org Admin Approval

The first Org Admin should be approved by the school.

The first Org Admin may be:

- Principal / School Head
- Officially designated school coordinator
- Guidance counselor/designate
- Learner Rights and Protection focal person
- ICT coordinator
- Another staff member formally approved by the principal or school head

The app provider should not independently decide who should control the school environment. The school should identify and approve the first Org Admin.

---
## Org Admin Activation

The first Org Admin should receive a unique activation invitation.

The invitation should be:

- Created only after school approval
- Assigned to the approved person
- Unique
- Time-limited
- Usable only once
- Not publicly shareable
- Recorded in the audit log
- Revocable if sent to the wrong person or no longer valid

When the approved Org Admin activates the account, the system should verify that:

1. The invitation exists.
2. The invitation has not expired.
3. The invitation has not already been used.
4. The invitation is assigned to the approved school organization.
5. The invitation grants only the role approved for that person.

---

## Org Admin Responsibilities

The Org Admin may help manage the school’s use of Speakup Connect.

Possible Org Admin responsibilities include:

- Managing the school profile
- Inviting approved staff users
- Assigning staff roles
- Uploading or managing approved student rosters
- Generating one-time activation codes for imported Student Login IDs
- Deactivating users who are no longer authorized
- Reviewing user access
- Coordinating with school leadership
- Supporting compliance with school-approved privacy and child protection procedures

The Org Admin should not override school policy. The Org Admin should operate under the authority of the school head or designated school leadership.

---

## Platform Provider Responsibilities

The app provider should be responsible for:

1. Creating the school organization only after reasonable verification.
2. Issuing the first Org Admin invitation only to the school-approved person.
3. Preventing public self-creation of school organizations.
4. Preventing public self-assignment of Org Admin roles.
5. Maintaining audit logs for school organization creation and first Org Admin activation.
6. Providing technical safeguards for school-controlled access.
7. Supporting role-based permissions.
8. Providing tools for user deactivation, roster management, and secure onboarding.

The app provider should not decide school policy, child protection procedures, report handling rules, or who should access sensitive student reports. Those decisions should remain with the school.

---

## Important Security Rule

No one should be able to become the Org Admin of a school simply by:

- Downloading the app
- Typing the school name
- Claiming to be from the school
- Entering publicly known school information
- Creating an account with a personal email address
- Selecting “I am an Admin”

Org Admin access should require school verification, school approval, and a controlled invitation process.

---

## Initial Org Admin Control

Because Speakup Connect may later be used by multiple schools, each school should have its own protected school environment within the platform.

No person should be able to download the app, select or type the name of a school, and make themselves the administrator of that school.

For MONHS, the first Org Admin account should be created only after MONHS has verified and approved the person who will serve in that role. The app provider would then issue a secure, one-time Org Admin invitation assigned to that approved person.

After activation, the approved Org Admin may help manage staff invitations, student rosters, student activation codes, and onboarding activities under MONHS authority and in accordance with MONHS-approved privacy and child protection procedures.

---

## Bottom Line

The first Org Admin setup is the foundation of secure onboarding.

Before students or staff are added, the school itself must be verified, and the first Org Admin must be approved by the school.

The app provider creates the technical school environment, but the school decides who is authorized to control it.

# Core Security Principle

## No Open Access to a School Environment

Speakup Connect may be publicly available for download from the App Store. However, installing the app must not automatically give anyone access to MONHS or to any other school’s private student reporting system.

Access to each school’s system should be controlled separately by that school through approved onboarding, roster verification, activation codes, staff approval, and role-based permissions.

All accounts should be controlled through one of the following:

1. Official MONHS student roster
2. Official MONHS staff list
3. School-approved invitation process
4. School-approved account activation
5. Role-based access permissions

A person should not gain access just because they know the school name, a student name, a grade level, or a section.

---

# Recommended Student Onboarding Model

## Best Approach: Roster-Based Student Activation

MONHS should provide or approve an official list of students who are allowed to use Speakup Connect.

The student roster should include only the information needed for account verification, such as:

- Student name
- School-provided Student Login ID
- Student ID or LRN, if approved by MONHS
- Grade level
- Section
- Adviser or class assignment
- Enrollment status

The app should use this roster as an allowlist.

This means:

- A student can only activate an account if they are on the MONHS roster.
- A student not on the roster cannot activate an account.
- A former student, transferred student, or outsider cannot create an account unless MONHS adds or reactivates them.
- The app does not decide who is a MONHS student; MONHS does.

---

## Student Onboarding Workflow

Before a student can activate a Speakup Connect account, MONHS must first approve the student through the official school roster or pilot roster.

During student onboarding:

1. MONHS approves the pilot roster or student roster.
2. Student Login IDs are provided by the school and imported into Speakup Connect. These are typically the school-assigned Student ID.
3. Speakup Connect generates a one-time activation code for each approved student.
4. MONHS distributes the Student Login ID and one-time activation code privately, preferably in person.
5. Student opens the app.
6. Student selects MONHS or scans a MONHS QR code.
7. Student chooses “Activate Student Account.”
8. Student enters the Student Login ID and one-time activation code.
9. Student creates a password, passphrase, or approved PIN.
10. The activation code becomes invalid after use.
11. The student account is linked to the approved roster record.

After activation:

- The student logs in using the school, Student Login ID, and password/PIN.
- The student does not need an email address.
- The student cannot create a MONHS account unless MONHS has already approved that student.
---
## School QR Code Use

The app may allow a student to scan a school QR code to select the correct school environment.

The QR code should only identify the school environment. It should not grant access by itself.

After scanning the QR code, the student must still enter the Student Login ID and one-time activation code issued by the school.

This prevents someone from gaining access simply because they saw or copied the school QR code.

---
## Student Initial Login Without Requiring Email

Not all students have email addresses, so Speakup Connect should not require email as the only way for students to activate or log in.

The recommended approach is to use a school-provided Student Login ID and a one-time activation code generated by Speakup Connect.

Speakup Connect will provide a Student Login ID import tool and an automatic one-time activation code generation tool. This allows the school to import its approved student roster and have Speakup Connect assign a one-time activation code to each imported Student Login ID.

For each approved student, the system should store or generate:

- School-provided Student Login ID imported from the approved roster
- One-time activation code generated by Speakup Connect
- School organization assignment
- Expiration date for the activation code

The Student Login ID is provided by the school and stored in the app. The app should not simply expose the student’s name, LRN, birthdate, or other sensitive information unless MONHS specifically chooses to use one of those identifiers.

The Student Login ID and one-time activation code should be distributed by MONHS in person through an adviser, coordinator, or supervised onboarding session.
---

## Automated Activation Code / PIN Generation

Speakup Connect can provide an automated service for generating student activation codes or PINs.

The school should be able to choose the format and length of these codes based on its preferred level of security and ease of use.

Examples include:

- 6-digit numeric code, such as `483921`
- 8-digit numeric code, such as `73918462`
- 8-character mixed code, such as `K7P4M9Q2`
- 10-character mixed code, such as `A8K2-P9M4Q7`

The system should generate these codes randomly and securely. The codes should not be based on student names, birthdates, grade levels, sections, or other easily guessed information.

Each activation code should be:

- Unique to one student
- Usable only once
- Time-limited, if possible
- Disabled after activation
- Revocable by authorized school staff
- Stored securely by the system
- Distributed privately by the school

MONHS or any future participating school can choose the activation code format that best balances security, simplicity, and student usability.

---

## Student Account Recovery Without Requiring Email

If a student forgets the password or changes device, account recovery should be handled through school verification.

The recommended recovery process is:

1. Student reports the problem to the adviser or designated school staff.
2. Staff verifies the student identity in person.
3. Staff generates a new one-time recovery code.
4. Student uses the recovery code to set a new password, passphrase, or approved PIN.
5. Existing sessions are revoked.
6. The reset action is recorded in the audit log.

Students should not be able to reset accounts using only easily guessed personal information.

---

## School Preparation Before Student Activation

Before students begin activating accounts, MONHS should complete the school-side preparation steps.

Recommended preparation steps:

1. MONHS approves the student roster or pilot roster.
2. MONHS decides which students are included in the pilot.
3. MONHS provides the Student Login IDs as part of the approved roster.
4. Speakup Connect imports the approved Student Login IDs.
5. Speakup Connect generates one-time activation codes for the imported Student Login IDs.
6. MONHS privately distributes the Student Login IDs and activation codes, preferably in person.
7. Students activate their own accounts using the Student Onboarding Workflow.
8. Unused, lost, or expired activation codes are disabled or regenerated as needed.

The school should decide:

- Which students are included in the pilot
- Which grade levels or sections are included
- Whether SSLG officers are included first
- Whether classroom presidents or selected student leaders are included first
- When the app will be expanded to more students

Activation codes should be distributed by MONHS, not publicly posted or shared online.

Recommended distribution methods:

- Given personally by the class adviser
- Given during a supervised classroom onboarding session
- Given to SSLG officers or pilot users in person
- Printed on individual slips
- Distributed only after the student’s identity is confirmed by school personnel

The Student Login ID and activation code should not be sent in a group chat, posted on Facebook, or shared in a public announcement.

Once the student activates the account:

- The activation code becomes invalid.
- Another user cannot use the same code.
- The same roster record cannot be claimed again without school approval.
- Any duplicate claim attempt should be blocked or flagged.

# Preventing One Student from Logging in as Another Student

The system should use several layers of protection.

## 1. One-Time Activation Codes

Each student receives a unique one-time activation code.

If another student tries to use the code after it has already been claimed, the attempt fails.

---

## 2. Roster Matching

The activation code must match the correct roster record.

A student should not be able to use another student’s name with their own code.

---

## 3. No Public Search of Student Records

The app should not allow students to search for names from the roster.

If an activation attempt fails, the app should not reveal whether the student name, ID, or code was the incorrect part.

Use a general message such as:

“Account activation could not be completed. Please contact your adviser or the school coordinator.”

This helps prevent someone from guessing valid student information.

---

## 4. Password or Passcode Protection

After activation, each student should create a password or secure login method.

Recommended rules:

- Minimum password length
- Block very weak passwords
- Do not allow passwords such as `123456`, `password`, or the student’s name
- Hide password entry
- Store passwords securely through a trusted authentication system
- Never store plain-text passwords

---

## 5. Rate Limiting

The app should limit repeated failed attempts.

For example:

- Limit repeated login attempts
- Limit repeated activation attempts
- Temporarily block suspicious repeated failures
- Log repeated failed attempts for review

This helps prevent guessing attacks.

---

## 6. Device and Session Controls

The app can add protection for suspicious access.

Possible controls:

- Notify or flag login from a new device
- Require re-authentication after a long period of inactivity
- Allow logout from all devices
- Prevent multiple suspicious sessions if needed
- Require additional verification for account recovery

---

## 7. Secure Account Recovery

Account recovery is a common weak point.

Students should not be able to reset an account simply by answering easy personal questions.

Recommended student account recovery process:

1. Student reports the problem to the adviser or designated school staff.
2. Staff verifies the student identity in person.
3. Staff resets the account or issues a new one-time recovery code.
4. The old sessions are revoked.
5. The reset action is logged.

This prevents another student from taking over an account through weak recovery questions.

---

# Staff Onboarding Plan

Staff accounts require stronger controls because staff may view confidential student reports.

## Staff Account Creation

Staff accounts should be created only after MONHS approval.

A staff member should not be able to self-register as an administrator.

MONHS should decide:

- Who receives staff access
- Who receives admin access
- Who can view reports
- Who can respond to reports
- Who can escalate reports
- Who can manage users
- Who can export or print reports, if allowed

---

## Staff Roles

Recommended staff roles:

### Principal / School Head

May view serious or escalated reports and oversee governance.

### SSLG Coordinator

May assist with student orientation and app implementation. Access to confidential reports should be limited based on MONHS decision.

### Guidance Counselor / Guidance Designate

May review and handle student welfare reports.

### Learner Rights and Protection / Child Protection Focal Person

May review sensitive child protection reports.

### Teacher / Adviser

May receive only reports assigned to them or reports appropriate to their role.

### Technical Administrator

May manage technical settings but should not routinely view confidential report contents unless authorized.

---

## Staff Login Security

Staff accounts should use stronger security than student accounts.

Recommended controls:

- No open staff registration
- School-approved account creation
- Strong password requirement
- Multi-factor authentication where practical
- Role-based permissions
- Audit logs for report access
- Immediate account deactivation when staff leave or change roles
- Principal or designated admin approval for high-level access

---

# No Activation Code / Help Request Process

If a student does not have an activation code, the app should not allow the student to create an account or request direct access.

Instead, the app may show a help message such as:

“Please contact your adviser, SSLG coordinator, or designated school staff to receive your activation code.”

This should not create an active account, pending account, or access request inside the system unless MONHS specifically approves that workflow.

The purpose is to prevent students, outsiders, former students, or unauthorized users from requesting access directly through the app without school verification.

---

# Recommended Pilot Onboarding Method

For the first pilot, the safest approach is a supervised onboarding session.

## Suggested Process

1. MONHS selects the pilot group.
2. MONHS approves the roster for that pilot group.
3. MONHS provides Student Login IDs for the approved students.
4. Speakup Connect imports the school-provided Student Login IDs and generates one-time activation codes for the approved students.
5. Student Login IDs and activation codes are printed or prepared individually.
6. Adviser or coordinator distributes the Student Login IDs and activation codes in person.
7. Students activate accounts during a supervised session.
8. Staff confirms that each student successfully activated only their own account.
9. Unused codes are collected, expired, or disabled.
10. Any failed or suspicious activation attempts are reviewed.

This is safer than letting students register on their own outside school supervision.
---

# Handling Special Cases

## Student Loses Activation Code

- Student reports to adviser or designated coordinator.
- Staff verifies identity.
- Old code is disabled.
- New one-time code is issued.
- Action is logged.

## Student Forgets Password

- Student reports to adviser or designated coordinator.
- Staff verifies identity.
- Password reset or recovery code is issued.
- Existing sessions are revoked.
- Action is logged.

## Student Changes Phone

- Student reports the change.
- Staff may issue a reactivation or recovery code.
- Old device sessions are revoked.

## Student Transfers Out

- MONHS marks the student inactive.
- Student login is disabled.
- Records are retained or archived based on MONHS policy.

## Student Transfers In

- MONHS adds the student to the approved roster.
- MONHS provides the Student Login ID for the new student.
- Speakup Connect generates an activation code for that Student Login ID.
- Student activates through the standard process.

## Student Tries to Use Another Student’s Account

- Failed attempts are logged.
- Repeated attempts are flagged.
- MONHS reviews the incident.
- If needed, the school handles it under its student conduct policy.

# Audit Logs

The app should keep audit logs for important actions.

Recommended audit logs:

- Account activation
- Failed activation attempts
- Login attempts
- Password reset
- Account recovery
- Staff viewing a report
- Staff changing report status
- Staff assigning or escalating a report
- User role changes
- Account deactivation
- Data export or printing, if allowed

Audit logs help MONHS detect misuse and demonstrate responsible handling of sensitive information.

---

# Data Privacy Considerations

The onboarding system should follow data minimization.

The app should collect only what is needed to verify identity and manage access.

Recommended privacy controls:

- Do not collect unnecessary personal information.
- Do not publicly expose the student roster.
- Do not reveal whether a specific student is in the roster during failed activation.
- Restrict access to roster data.
- Restrict access to report data.
- Use role-based permissions.
- Deactivate accounts when students or staff are no longer authorized.
- Provide a privacy notice before or during onboarding.

---

# Technical Safeguards to Implement

The app should include the following technical protections:

1. Roster-based allowlist
2. One-time activation codes
3. Expiring invitation codes
4. Secure password handling
5. Rate limiting for failed attempts
6. Generic error messages
7. Role-based access control
8. Staff approval for admin accounts
9. Audit logs
10. Account lockout or temporary delay after repeated failures
11. Secure account recovery
12. Ability to deactivate users
13. Ability to revoke sessions
14. Protection against duplicate account claims
15. Protection against direct database access from the app
16. Encryption in transit
17. Secure server-side validation
18. Regular review of active users

---

# Firebase / Cloud Implementation Notes

If Speakup Connect uses Firebase or a similar backend, the following approach is recommended:

1. Use Firebase Authentication or another trusted authentication provider.
2. Store student roster records separately from user login records.
3. Do not allow the client app to directly approve its own access.
4. Use server-side functions to validate activation codes.
5. Use custom claims or role records to control permissions.
6. Use security rules to prevent students from reading other students’ data.
7. Use security rules to prevent general staff from viewing restricted reports.
8. Store activation codes securely, preferably as hashed values.
9. Use App Check or similar protections where available.
10. Keep logs of sensitive account and report actions.

The app should not trust the mobile app alone to decide who is allowed access. Important decisions should be enforced on the server side.

Students would receive Student Login IDs and unique one-time activation codes distributed by the school, preferably in person. # Bottom Line

Secure onboarding should be based on school verification first and app enforcement second.

The app should not decide who belongs to MONHS. MONHS should decide that through the official roster.

The app should then enforce that decision by using:

- Roster-based access
- One-time activation codes
- No open access to a school environment
- Role-based permissions
- Strong staff authentication
- Secure account recovery
- Audit logs
- Account deactivation controls

This approach gives MONHS control, protects student privacy, and reduces the risk of unauthorized access or impersonation.
