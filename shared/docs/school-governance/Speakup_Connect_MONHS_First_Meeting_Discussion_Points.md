# Speakup Connect MONHS First Meeting Discussion Points

## Purpose

Discuss whether MONHS is willing to move forward with a controlled pilot of Speakup Connect while addressing privacy, child protection, secure onboarding, staff responsibilities, and student safety.

Speakup Connect is intended to support MONHS procedures. It does not replace school authority, DepEd procedures, guidance personnel, child protection processes, or school leadership decisions.

---

## Key Concerns Being Addressed

Speakup Connect may involve student information and sensitive reports, so the implementation should address:

- Philippine Data Privacy Act considerations
- DepEd Child Protection Policy considerations
- Confidential handling of student reports
- Secure student and staff onboarding
- Role-based access to reports
- Clear responsibility for report review and escalation
- Staff/admin training before student rollout
- Student and/or parent privacy notice, terms of use, and disclaimer

---

## Proposed Controlled Pilot Approach

A limited pilot is recommended before any wider rollout.

Suggested approach:

1. MONHS identifies the official point persons.
2. MONHS confirms who should have staff/admin access.
3. MONHS approves the initial student pilot group.
4. Speakup Connect is configured for MONHS.
5. Staff/admin users are trained first.
6. Students are onboarded only after MONHS is ready.
7. MONHS reviews the pilot before deciding whether to expand.

Possible pilot groups:

- SSLG officers
- Classroom presidents
- Selected student leaders
- One grade level or limited student group approved by MONHS

---

## Secure Onboarding Summary

The app may be publicly available for download, but access to MONHS must be school-controlled.

Student onboarding:

1. MONHS approves the pilot roster or student roster.
2. Student Login IDs are provided by the school and imported into Speakup Connect.
3. Speakup Connect generates one-time activation codes/PINs.
4. MONHS privately distributes the Student Login ID and activation code.
5. Students activate their own accounts.
6. No student can join MONHS in the app unless MONHS has approved them.

Staff/Admin onboarding:

- Staff accounts require MONHS approval.
- The first Org Admin must be approved by MONHS.
- No one can download the app and make themselves an administrator.
- Staff access should be based on role and need-to-know responsibility.

---

## Privacy and Child Protection Safeguards

Speakup Connect can support MONHS governance through:

- Role-based access
- Restricted handling of sensitive reports
- Report categories and routing
- Confidentiality controls
- First-time privacy notice, terms of use, and disclaimer
- Audit logs where practical
- Secure account recovery
- Staff/admin training
- School-approved escalation procedures

Sensitive reports should be visible only to authorized MONHS personnel.

SSLG may help promote awareness and responsible use, but SSLG officers should not automatically have access to confidential student reports.

---

## Decisions Needed From MONHS

The most important decisions are:

1. Who will be the official MONHS point person?
2. Who should be the first Org Admin?
3. Who should have staff/admin access?
4. Who should review student reports?
5. Who should handle sensitive child protection reports?
6. What report categories should be included?
7. Which report categories should be restricted?
8. What student group should be included in the pilot?
9. How should students receive activation codes?
10. Should student and/or parent notices be prepared before launch?
11. When should staff/admin training be scheduled?

---

## Suggested Next Steps

If MONHS is interested in moving forward:

1. MONHS reviews the privacy, child protection, and secure onboarding documents.
2. MONHS identifies the point persons and first Org Admin.
3. MONHS approves the initial pilot group.
4. Speakup Connect is configured for MONHS.
5. Staff/admin training is scheduled.
6. Student onboarding begins only after MONHS approval.

---

## Desired Meeting Outcome

A successful meeting does not need to result in full approval on the spot.

A successful meeting would result in:

- Agreement that the privacy and child protection concerns are understood
- Agreement that Speakup Connect should only move forward as a controlled pilot
- Identification of MONHS decision-makers and point persons
- Agreement on what must be reviewed before launch
- A clear next step for training, configuration, or pilot planning
