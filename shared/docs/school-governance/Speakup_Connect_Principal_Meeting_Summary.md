# Speakup Connect: Principal Meeting Summary

## Purpose of This Meeting

Speakup Connect is being proposed as a student-support and reporting tool for MONHS. The goal is to help students report concerns in a structured way while allowing authorized school personnel to review, respond, and escalate concerns responsibly.

Because the app may involve student information and sensitive reports, it must be implemented with awareness of:

- The Philippine Data Privacy Act of 2012
- DepEd Child Protection Policy and related supplemental guidelines
- MONHS-approved privacy, access, reporting, and escalation procedures

This meeting is intended to confirm that these concerns are understood and that the app will be implemented through a controlled, school-approved pilot rather than an informal launch.

---

## Main Position

Speakup Connect should support MONHS procedures. It should not replace school authority, DepEd procedures, guidance personnel, child protection processes, or school leadership decisions.

MONHS should define who is authorized to access student reports, who responds to reports, which reports require escalation, and how privacy and child protection requirements will be applied.

The app provider can provide the technical tools, secure onboarding, role-based access, configuration support, and training needed to help MONHS apply those rules inside the app.

---

## Key Privacy and Child Protection Concerns

Speakup Connect may collect or process information such as:

- Student name or school identifier
- Grade level and section
- Report details
- Names of other students, staff, or witnesses
- Supporting screenshots or files
- School response notes and status updates

Some reports may involve sensitive matters such as bullying, abuse, harassment, violence, discrimination, emotional distress, online safety, or child protection concerns.

Because of this, the app should be implemented with:

1. Clear purpose
2. Limited data collection
3. Restricted access
4. Confidential handling
5. Secure onboarding
6. Role-based permissions
7. School-approved report routing
8. School-approved escalation workflow
9. Audit logs where practical
10. Training before wider rollout

---

## School Responsibilities

MONHS should remain responsible for governance decisions, including:

1. Approving whether and how the app will be used.
2. Identifying the official school point persons.
3. Defining who may view and respond to reports.
4. Identifying the guidance counselor/designate and child protection focal person.
5. Deciding which report categories are allowed.
6. Deciding which report types are sensitive or restricted.
7. Defining the official escalation process.
8. Approving student and parent privacy notices, if required.
9. Approving the student roster or pilot roster.
10. Approving staff and admin users.
11. Deciding when and how the pilot expands.

---

## App Provider / Technical Support Responsibilities

The app provider should be responsible for technical support and configuration, including:

1. Configuring the MONHS school environment.
2. Setting up role-based access.
3. Supporting secure student and staff onboarding.
4. Generating student login IDs and activation codes.
5. Helping configure report categories and routing.
6. Supporting restricted handling of sensitive reports.
7. Helping prepare onboarding and training materials.
8. Providing admin training.
9. Supporting audit logs and account controls where practical.
10. Avoiding routine access to confidential report contents unless authorized and necessary for support.

The app provider should not decide school policy, disciplinary action, child protection decisions, or final report handling procedures.

---

## Secure Access and Onboarding

Speakup Connect may be publicly available in the App Store, but installing the app must not automatically give anyone access to MONHS or any other school’s private system.

Each school should have its own protected school environment. Access should be controlled by that school.

For MONHS:

- Students should only activate accounts if they are on the MONHS-approved roster or pilot roster.
- Staff and admin users should only receive access after MONHS approval.
- No one should be able to download the app, select MONHS, and make themselves a student, staff member, or administrator.
- No one should be able to create a school environment or become Org Admin without school verification and approval.

---

## Student Onboarding Summary

Students should not need email addresses to use the app.

Recommended student onboarding:

1. MONHS approves the pilot roster or student roster.
2. Student Login IDs are provided by the school and imported into Speakup Connect 
3. Speakup Connect generates a one-time activation code for each approved student.
4. MONHS distributes the Student Login ID and one-time activation code privately, preferably in person.
5. Student opens the app.
6. Student selects MONHS or scans a MONHS QR code.
7. Student chooses “Activate Student Account.”
8. Student enters the Student Login ID and one-time activation code.
9. Student creates a password, passphrase, or approved PIN.
10. The activation code becomes invalid after use.
11. The student account is linked to the approved roster record.

The school may choose the activation code format, such as a 6-digit numeric code, 8-digit numeric code, or mixed-character code.

---

## Staff and Org Admin Onboarding Summary

Staff accounts require stronger control because staff may access confidential student reports.

Recommended approach:

- Staff accounts should not be self-created.
- MONHS should approve each staff user.
- Roles should be assigned based on school responsibility.
- Sensitive report access should be limited to authorized personnel.
- The first Org Admin should be created only after MONHS verifies and approves the person.
- The app provider should issue a secure, one-time Org Admin invitation to the approved person.

The Org Admin may help manage staff invitations, student rosters, activation codes, and onboarding under MONHS authority.

---

## Report Access and Escalation

Reports should be handled on a need-to-know basis.

Possible routing:

- Facility or general concerns: assigned school administrator or responsible staff
- Bullying or student welfare concerns: guidance/designated personnel
- Sensitive child protection concerns: principal, guidance counselor/designate, and Learner Rights and Protection / Child Protection focal person

SSLG may help promote awareness and responsible use, but SSLG officers should not automatically have access to confidential student reports.

---

## Recommended Pilot Approach

A controlled pilot is recommended before any school-wide launch.

Suggested phases:

1. Governance confirmation
2. Privacy and child protection setup
3. Admin/staff training
4. Limited student pilot
5. Review and adjustment
6. Wider rollout only if MONHS is ready

This allows MONHS to test the workflow, confirm responsibilities, adjust report categories, and make sure sensitive information is handled properly before expanding.

---

## Key Decisions Needed From MONHS

The most important meeting outcomes are:

1. Who will be the official MONHS owner or point person?
2. Who should be the first Org Admin?
3. Who should have staff/admin access?
4. Who should handle student reports?
5. Who should handle sensitive child protection reports?
6. What report categories should be included?
7. Which categories should be restricted?
8. What student group should be included in the pilot?
9. How should students receive activation codes?
10. When should admin training be scheduled?

---

## Bottom Line

The Privacy Act and Child Protection Policy concerns are valid and should be treated as central to the implementation plan.

Speakup Connect can move forward responsibly if it is introduced as a school-controlled pilot with:

- MONHS-approved governance
- Secure onboarding
- Role-based access
- Restricted handling of sensitive reports
- Clear escalation procedures
- Staff training before student rollout

The app is a tool to support MONHS. The school remains responsible for official decisions, report handling, privacy governance, and child protection procedures.
