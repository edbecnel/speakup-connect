# Speakup Connect: First-Time Privacy, Terms of Use, and Disclaimer Requirements

## Purpose

Before an administrator, staff member, or student can use the Speakup Connect app or website, the user should be presented with and required to acknowledge or accept the applicable privacy, usage, and responsibility documents.

These requirements should apply during first-time account activation. The current documents should remain available afterward from **Settings > Privacy and Legal**.

The final documents and acceptance process should be reviewed and approved by the MONHS Data Protection Officer and appropriate legal or DepEd personnel before launch.

## Required Documents

Speakup Connect should present three separate documents.

### 1. Privacy Notice

The Privacy Notice should clearly explain:

- What personal and sensitive information is collected
- Why the information is collected and processed
- The lawful basis for processing
- How the information is processed and protected
- Who may access or receive the information
- Where the information is stored
- How long it will be retained
- When information may be disclosed or escalated because of a safety concern, school procedure, or legal obligation
- The user's rights as a data subject
- How those rights may be exercised
- The identities and contact information of the responsible school and Speakup Connect privacy contacts
- Whether automated processing, profiling, or automated decision-making is used

The Privacy Notice should identify the respective privacy roles of MONHS and the Speakup Connect provider, including which organization acts as the Personal Information Controller and which acts as the Personal Information Processor. These roles should also be established contractually.

The National Privacy Commission states that a data subject must be informed about the processing of personal data. When processing is not based on consent, the applicable lawful basis must be identified. A Privacy Notice is not automatically a consent form.

Reference: [National Privacy Commission: Right to Be Informed](https://privacy.gov.ph/the-right-to-be-informed/)

### 2. Terms of Use

The Terms of Use should address:

- Permitted and prohibited uses of Speakup Connect
- Account and password security
- Prohibition against sharing accounts or credentials
- Expectations for truthful, good-faith use
- Prohibition against knowingly false, malicious, threatening, or abusive submissions
- Prohibition against attempting to access another user's account or information
- Appropriate use of confidential information
- Conditions under which an account may be suspended, restricted, or terminated
- Procedures for reporting suspected account misuse or security incidents

The wording should not discourage students from making good-faith reports. A student should not be penalized merely because a sincerely submitted report cannot later be substantiated.

### 3. Responsibilities and Safety Disclaimer

The Responsibilities and Safety Disclaimer should clearly distinguish the responsibilities of Speakup Connect, the school, and the user.

#### Speakup Connect Responsibilities

Speakup Connect is responsible for:

- Providing and maintaining the reporting and communication platform
- Implementing reasonable technical and organizational security safeguards
- Providing role-based access controls
- Protecting information according to the approved privacy and security design
- Providing technical support and notifying the school of relevant security incidents
- Supporting the school's authorized reporting and escalation workflow

Speakup Connect provides the technical platform. It does not independently investigate school incidents, determine disciplinary outcomes, provide counseling, or replace the school's child-protection responsibilities.

#### School Responsibilities

MONHS is responsible for:

- Determining which personnel are authorized to receive and review reports
- Defining report-handling and escalation procedures
- Reviewing and responding to submitted reports
- Following applicable DepEd child-protection, privacy, and school policies
- Protecting information accessed or exported by authorized personnel
- Deactivating access when a staff member is no longer authorized
- Providing students and parents or guardians with required notices
- Determining when parent or guardian consent is required

#### User Responsibilities

Users are responsible for:

- Protecting their login credentials and devices
- Not sharing accounts or passwords
- Using Speakup Connect in good faith
- Not knowingly submitting false, malicious, or abusive information
- Respecting the privacy and rights of other people
- Promptly reporting suspected account misuse or unauthorized access
- Following applicable school rules and instructions

#### Safety Limitations

The disclaimer should clearly state:

- Speakup Connect is not an emergency response service.
- Speakup Connect is not a police, medical, or counseling service.
- Submitting a report does not guarantee an immediate response or a particular outcome.
- A person facing immediate danger should contact emergency services, school authorities, a trusted adult, or another appropriate source of immediate assistance.
- Confidentiality cannot be guaranteed when limited disclosure is necessary to protect a person, comply with law, or follow an approved child-protection procedure.

No disclaimer should attempt to waive the legal responsibilities of Speakup Connect, MONHS, or the user.

## Separate Acknowledgment and Acceptance

The activation screen should use separate, unchecked confirmations:

```text
[ ] I acknowledge that I have read and understood the Privacy Notice.

[ ] I agree to the Terms of Use.

[ ] I understand the Responsibilities and Safety Disclaimer.
```

Required acknowledgment of the Privacy Notice should not be described as consent to every kind of processing. When consent is the lawful basis for a particular processing activity, consent should be freely given, specific, informed, recorded, and separate from unrelated terms.

Optional processing consent should:

- Be presented separately
- Explain the specific optional purpose
- Not be selected by default
- Allow the user to refuse without losing unrelated services
- Explain how consent may be withdrawn

Reference: [Republic Act No. 10173, Data Privacy Act of 2012](https://privacy.gov.ph/data-privacy-act/)

## Student and Minor Requirements

Students should receive a short, plain-language summary appropriate for their age, followed by access to the complete documents.

Because many students are minors:

- MONHS should determine when parent or guardian notice, acknowledgment, or consent is required.
- A student's acknowledgment should not replace a required parent or guardian process.
- The student should still receive an age-appropriate explanation and be asked for appropriate acknowledgment or assent.
- The language should be clear, accessible, and available in the languages reasonably needed by students and families.
- The wording should not frighten or discourage students from making good-faith reports.
- Students should understand that authorized school personnel may review a report and may escalate limited information when safety, school procedure, or law requires it.

The National Privacy Commission notes that parents or legal guardians are responsible for asserting the privacy rights of minors under their care.

Reference: [National Privacy Commission: Data Subject Rights](https://privacy.gov.ph/data-subject-rights/)

## Timing of the Privacy Notice

The first-login notice is necessary, but it may not be sufficient by itself.

MONHS will provide and import student roster information before a student activates a Speakup Connect account. Therefore, the student and, when applicable, the parent or guardian should receive the Privacy Notice before roster import or at the earliest practical opportunity.

The recommended sequence is:

1. MONHS provides the applicable Privacy Notice to students and parents or guardians.
2. Any legally or institutionally required consent or authorization is obtained and recorded.
3. MONHS approves and imports the student roster and school-assigned Student Login IDs.
4. Speakup Connect generates the one-time activation codes.
5. At first activation, the student receives the plain-language notice and completes the required acknowledgments and acceptance.

Administrators and staff should complete the same first-login process before receiving access to school information or administrative features.

## Acceptance Records

Speakup Connect should securely record the following for each required document:

- User ID
- School organization ID
- User role
- Document type
- Document title and version
- Document hash or other integrity identifier
- Date and time presented
- Date and time acknowledged or accepted, using a trusted server timestamp
- App or website version
- Language presented
- Acceptance method
- Parent or guardian status, when applicable

The system should not collect an IP address solely for acceptance evidence unless MONHS and Speakup Connect determine that it is necessary, proportionate, disclosed in the Privacy Notice, and properly protected.

## Document Changes

Each document should have a version number and effective date.

Users should be required to review and accept a new version when a material change affects matters such as:

- Information collected
- Purposes or lawful bases for processing
- Parties receiving information
- Retention periods
- User responsibilities
- Safety escalation procedures
- Available rights or methods for exercising them

Minor editorial corrections that do not change the meaning may be recorded without forcing unnecessary reacceptance.

Previous versions and their acceptance records should be retained according to the approved records-retention policy.

## Additional Organizational Requirement

Individual user acceptance does not replace the need for a written agreement between MONHS and the Speakup Connect provider.

The organizational agreement should establish:

- Privacy roles and responsibilities
- Authorized processing instructions
- Security responsibilities
- Confidentiality obligations
- Data retention and deletion requirements
- Security incident and breach procedures
- Support and service responsibilities
- Termination and data-return procedures
- Restrictions on secondary use of school and student information

## Implementation Summary

Before accessing Speakup Connect, each administrator, staff member, and student should:

1. Receive the appropriate Privacy Notice.
2. Acknowledge that the Privacy Notice was read and understood.
3. Accept the Terms of Use.
4. Acknowledge the Responsibilities and Safety Disclaimer.
5. Complete any separate consent process that is legally required.
6. Have the acceptance recorded against the applicable document versions.

This process provides transparency and accountability, but it does not replace MONHS approval, a lawful basis for processing, required parent or guardian involvement, or the organizational agreement between MONHS and the Speakup Connect provider.
