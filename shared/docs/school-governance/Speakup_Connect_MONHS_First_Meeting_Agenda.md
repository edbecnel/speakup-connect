# Speakup Connect MONHS First Meeting Agenda

## Meeting Purpose

Discuss whether and how MONHS can move forward with a controlled pilot of Speakup Connect while addressing privacy, child protection, secure onboarding, staff responsibilities, and student safety.

---

## 1. Opening and Meeting Objective

- Thank the principal and SSLG coordinator for meeting.
- Acknowledge that the app was originally student-led and already demonstrated by the SSLG student president.
- Clarify that the purpose of this meeting is not just to demonstrate the app again, but to discuss responsible implementation.

**Main objective:**  
Determine whether MONHS is willing to move toward a controlled pilot and identify what must be addressed before launch.

---

## 2. Principal’s Concerns and Priorities

Ask the principal and SSLG coordinator to share their main concerns first.

Possible topics:

- Data Privacy Act
- DepEd Child Protection Policy
- Student safety
- Confidentiality
- Staff workload
- Who will manage reports
- Parent/student notification
- Pilot scope

---

## 3. Brief Overview of Speakup Connect

Keep this short.

Cover:

- What the app is intended to do
- What problem it helps solve
- How students may submit concerns
- How authorized school personnel may review and respond
- How SSLG can support awareness without automatically accessing confidential reports

---

## 4. Governance and Responsibility Model

Explain the key principle:

**Speakup Connect is a tool to support MONHS procedures. It does not replace school authority, DepEd procedures, guidance personnel, or child protection processes.**

Discuss:

- MONHS defines the official report-handling process.
- MONHS decides who can view and respond to reports.
- MONHS decides which reports require escalation.
- Speakup Connect provides the technical tools and configuration support.

---

## 5. Privacy and Child Protection Safeguards

Briefly explain how the app can support governance concerns:

- Role-based access
- Restricted handling of sensitive reports
- Report categories and routing
- Confidentiality controls
- Privacy notice and terms of use
- First-time user acceptance
- Audit logs where practical
- Training before student rollout

---

## 6. Secure Onboarding

Explain that the app may be publicly downloadable, but access to MONHS will be school-controlled.

Student onboarding:

1. MONHS approves the pilot roster or student roster.
2. Student Login IDs are provided by the school and imported into Speakup Connect.
3. Speakup Connect generates one-time activation codes/PINs.
4. MONHS privately distributes the Student Login ID and activation code.
5. Students activate their own accounts.
6. No student can join MONHS in the app unless MONHS has approved them.

Staff/Admin onboarding:

- Staff accounts require school approval.
- The first Org Admin must be approved by MONHS.
- No one can download the app and make themselves an administrator.

---

## 7. Proposed Pilot Approach

Recommend starting small.

Possible pilot group:

- SSLG officers
- Classroom presidents
- Selected student leaders
- One grade level or limited student group

Suggested phases:

1. Confirm governance and point persons.
2. Configure roles, categories, and privacy notices.
3. Train admin/staff users.
4. Launch limited student pilot.
5. Review and adjust before wider rollout.

---

## 8. Decisions Needed From MONHS

The most important decisions to request:

1. Who will be the official MONHS point person?
2. Who should be the first Org Admin?
3. Who should have staff/admin access?
4. Who should review student reports?
5. Who should handle sensitive child protection reports?
6. What report categories should be included?
7. What student group should be included in the pilot?
8. How should students receive activation codes?
9. Should parent/student notices be prepared before launch?
10. When should staff/admin training be scheduled?

---

## 9. Next Steps

Suggested next steps if MONHS is interested:

1. MONHS identifies point persons.
2. MONHS reviews the privacy, child protection, and secure onboarding documents.
3. MONHS approves the initial pilot group.
4. Speakup Connect is configured for MONHS.
5. Staff/admin training is scheduled.
6. Pilot onboarding begins only after MONHS is ready.

---

## Desired Meeting Outcome

The desired outcome is not necessarily full approval on the spot.

A successful meeting would result in:

- Agreement that the concerns are understood
- Agreement that the app should only move forward as a controlled pilot
- Identification of the MONHS decision-makers and point persons
- Agreement on what must be reviewed before launch
- A tentative next step for training, configuration, or pilot planning

---

## Suggested Opening Statement

“I prepared a short fallback agenda in case there is not already a planned agenda. I’m happy to follow your priorities first, but I wanted to make sure we cover privacy, child protection, secure onboarding, and what MONHS would need before considering a pilot.”
