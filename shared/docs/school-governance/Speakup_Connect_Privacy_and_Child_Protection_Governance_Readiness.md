# Speakup Connect: Privacy and Child Protection Governance Readiness

## Purpose of This Discussion

Speakup Connect is intended to support MONHS by giving students a structured and safer way to report concerns, request help, and communicate issues that may otherwise go unreported.

Because the users are learners, and because reports may involve bullying, abuse, harassment, safety, emotional distress, discrimination, or other sensitive concerns, the app must be implemented with awareness of the following governances:

- Philippine Data Privacy Act of 2012
  https://privacy.gov.ph/data-privacy-act/

- DepEd Child Protection Policy / Supplemental Guidelines
  https://www.depedncr.com.ph/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/RM-No.-1015-s.-2024-Supplemental-Guidelines-for-the-Implementation-of-DepEd-Ord-No.-40-s.-2012-or-the-DepEd-Child-Protection-Policy.pdf

The goal is not to bypass existing school procedures. The goal is to help MONHS apply those procedures in a more organized, trackable, and responsible way.

---

## My Understanding of the Data Privacy Act

The Data Privacy Act protects personal information in both government and private information systems. It applies when personal information is collected, recorded, stored, retrieved, used, disclosed, erased, or destroyed.

Speakup Connect may collect or process information such as:

- Student name
- Grade level
- Section
- Report details
- Names of other learners or personnel
- Screenshots or supporting files
- Witness details
- School response notes
- Status updates or follow-up actions

Some reports may involve sensitive personal information, especially when they involve age, education, health, emotional concerns, sexual matters, abuse, violence, bullying, or alleged offenses.

Because of this, Speakup Connect should follow the core privacy principles of:

### Transparency

Students and parents should know:

- What information is collected
- Why it is collected
- Who may access it
- How it will be used
- How long it will be retained
- Who to contact for privacy concerns

### Legitimate Purpose

The app should only be used for clearly defined school purposes, such as:

- Learner welfare
- Reporting concerns
- Guidance intervention
- Student safety
- Child protection
- Communication of school-related concerns

### Proportionality / Data Minimization

The app should collect only the information needed for the school to understand and act on the concern.

Students should not be required to provide unnecessary personal details.

### Security and Confidentiality

The app should include reasonable safeguards to help prevent:

- Unauthorized access
- Misuse of information
- Unnecessary disclosure
- Loss of student data
- Viewing of sensitive reports by people who do not need to know

## My Understanding of the School’s Responsibility

If MONHS decides to use Speakup Connect as an official school reporting tool, the school should define and control how the app is used.

The school should decide:

1. What types of reports the app may accept.
2. What information students are allowed or required to submit.
3. Who is authorized to view reports.
4. Who is responsible for responding to reports.
5. Which reports are guidance matters.
6. Which reports are discipline matters.
7. Which reports are child protection matters.
8. Which reports must be escalated under DepEd procedures.
9. How long report records should be retained.
10. Whether parent consent, student notice, or both are required before rollout.
11. Who the school’s Data Protection Officer or privacy focal person is.
12. Who the school’s Learner Rights and Protection / Child Protection focal persons are.

MONHS should retain governance control over:

- Access rules
- Report handling
- Escalation procedures
- Privacy notices
- Data retention
- Authorized users
- School response procedures

---

## My Understanding of My Responsibility

My role is to provide technical support and help configure Speakup Connect so that it supports MONHS’s privacy and child protection responsibilities.

I should not independently decide:

- School policy
- Disciplinary action
- Child protection procedures
- Which personnel are authorized to handle sensitive cases
- Which reports require outside referral
- Final decisions on report handling

Those decisions should come from MONHS leadership, guidance personnel, and the proper DepEd-designated focal persons.

My responsibilities should include:

1. Explaining clearly what the app collects and stores.
2. Configuring user roles and access permissions based on MONHS’s instructions.
3. Helping create a pilot implementation plan.
4. Supporting admin training.
5. Helping prepare a privacy notice for students and parents.
6. Helping configure report categories and routing.
7. Helping restrict sensitive reports to authorized personnel.
8. Helping document the report workflow.
9. Helping implement reasonable safeguards such as secure login, role-based access, and limited visibility.
10. Providing technical support without routinely viewing confidential report contents unless authorized and necessary.

If MONHS uses the app officially, my role should be treated as a technical support / system support role, not as the school authority responsible for deciding how child protection cases are handled.

## My Understanding of the DepEd Child Protection Policy

The DepEd Child Protection Policy and supplemental guidelines are directly relevant to Speakup Connect because the app may receive reports involving:

- Bullying
- Harassment
- Abuse
- Violence
- Discrimination
- Online safety concerns
- Emotional distress
- Threats
- Exploitation
- Neglect
- Other learner protection concerns

Speakup Connect should not be treated as a general chat tool. It should be treated as a structured school reporting channel with clear review, confidentiality, and escalation rules.

The app can help MONHS receive and organize reports, but the school must still follow DepEd procedures for handling child protection matters.

---

## Child Protection Responsibilities That Should Remain With MONHS

MONHS should determine the official process for handling reports.

The app can support the process, but the school should define and approve it.

Important school responsibilities include:

1. Identifying the Child Protection Committee members or equivalent designated personnel.
2. Identifying the Learner Rights and Protection focal person.
3. Identifying the guidance counselor or guidance designate.
4. Deciding who receives reports.
5. Deciding who classifies reports.
6. Deciding which cases require immediate escalation.
7. Deciding what information must be recorded in official forms or intake sheets.
8. Deciding when parents or guardians must be involved.
9. Deciding when the Schools Division Office, Regional Office, Central Office, social welfare office, police, or other agencies must be involved.
10. Ensuring confidential handling of sensitive student information.

Sensitive reports should not be visible to all teachers, SSLG officers, or general admin users.

## How Speakup Connect Can Help MONHS Apply These Governances

Speakup Connect can be configured to support the Data Privacy Act and Child Protection Policy in several ways.

---

## 1. Role-Based Access

Not everyone should see every report.

The app can support different user roles, such as:

- Student user
- SSLG awareness/support role, if approved
- Guidance counselor/designate
- Learner Rights and Protection focal person
- Principal/school head
- Technical administrator

Sensitive reports can be restricted to authorized personnel only.

This helps protect student privacy and supports proper handling of child protection concerns.

---

## 2. Report Category Routing

Reports can be categorized so that different types of concerns go to the correct school personnel.

Suggested categories include:

- Bullying
- Safety concern
- Harassment
- Discrimination
- Mental or emotional concern
- Facility concern
- Academic concern
- Online safety concern
- Child protection concern
- Other concern

Highly sensitive categories can be routed only to the principal, guidance counselor/designate, or child protection focal person.

---

## 3. Limited SSLG Access

Because this is a student-led initiative, SSLG can help with:

- Awareness
- Orientation
- Promotion of responsible use
- Encouraging students to report concerns appropriately
- Helping explain the purpose of the app

However, SSLG officers should not automatically have access to confidential student reports unless MONHS specifically approves a limited and appropriate role.

This protects both:

- The students submitting reports
- The SSLG officers themselves

---

## 4. Privacy Notice

Before rollout, MONHS can provide a simple student and parent privacy notice explaining:

- What information is collected
- Why it is collected
- Who can access it
- How reports are handled
- How long information is retained
- Who to contact for privacy concerns
- What happens in serious child protection cases

This helps demonstrate transparency and responsible handling of learner information.

---

## 5. Data Minimization

The app should only ask for information needed to understand and act on the report.

Possible approach:

- Keep required fields limited.
- Use optional fields where possible.
- Avoid collecting unnecessary personal details.
- Avoid giving broad access to report contents.
- Allow sensitive information to be routed only to authorized personnel.

---

## 6. Escalation Workflow

The app can help MONHS document a clear flow:

1. Student submits report.
2. Authorized reviewer receives it.
3. Reviewer classifies the concern.
4. Non-sensitive concerns are handled by the assigned school personnel.
5. Serious or sensitive concerns are escalated to the principal, guidance counselor/designate, and Learner Rights and Protection / Child Protection focal person.
6. If required, MONHS follows DepEd reporting and referral procedures.

The app should support the school’s official escalation process, not replace it.

---

## 7. Confidential Handling

Reports should be handled on a need-to-know basis.

Sensitive reports should not be visible to general users.

The system can help prevent unnecessary disclosure by limiting access based on role.

Examples:

- A facility concern may be visible to an assigned school administrator.
- A bullying report may be visible to guidance and designated school personnel.
- A child protection concern may be visible only to the principal, guidance counselor/designate, and Learner Rights and Protection focal person.
- SSLG may help promote the app but should not automatically view confidential reports.

---

## 8. Training Support

I can help train authorized MONHS personnel on:

- Logging in
- Reviewing reports
- Assigning or updating report status
- Recognizing sensitive reports
- Maintaining confidentiality
- Avoiding unauthorized sharing
- Following the school-approved escalation path
- Using the app without replacing official DepEd procedures

Admin training should happen before any wider student rollout.

---

# Recommended Implementation Approach

I recommend that MONHS begin with a controlled pilot instead of a full school-wide launch.

---

## Phase 1 — Governance Confirmation

MONHS identifies the official point persons, including:

- Principal
- SSLG coordinator
- Guidance counselor/designate
- Learner Rights and Protection focal person
- Child Protection Committee members or designated personnel
- Any other authorized personnel

---

## Phase 2 — Privacy and Child Protection Setup

MONHS reviews and approves:

- Report categories
- User roles
- Access permissions
- Escalation procedure
- Privacy notice
- Data retention approach
- Sensitive report handling
- Parent/student notification requirements

---

## Phase 3 — Admin Training

Only authorized school personnel are trained first.

The app should not be opened widely to students until the receiving team knows how to handle reports.

Training should include:

- How to use the dashboard
- How to review reports
- How to classify concerns
- How to protect confidentiality
- How to escalate sensitive reports
- What not to do with student information

---

## Phase 4 — Limited Pilot

The pilot could begin with:

- SSLG officers
- Classroom presidents
- Selected student leaders
- A limited grade level
- A small controlled group approved by MONHS

SSLG can help promote responsible use, but confidential reports should remain restricted to authorized personnel.

---

## Phase 5 — Review and Adjust

After a short pilot period, MONHS can review:

- What worked well
- What confused users
- Whether the report categories are correct
- Whether access permissions are appropriate
- Whether the workflow is manageable
- Whether additional training is needed
- Whether the app is ready for broader rollout

---

# Implementation Position

The Data Privacy Act and DepEd Child Protection Policy are important considerations for Speakup Connect because the app may involve student information and possibly sensitive reports.

For that reason, Speakup Connect should not be launched casually or opened widely without a clear school-approved implementation plan.

The recommended approach is for MONHS to treat Speakup Connect as a school-controlled pilot with:

- Clear access rules
- A privacy notice for students and parents
- Approved report categories
- Role-based permissions
- Restricted access to sensitive reports
- An approved child protection escalation workflow
- Training for authorized school personnel before wider student rollout

The app provider can help configure the system, support training, prepare onboarding guidance, and provide technical safeguards. However, MONHS should define who is authorized to view reports, who responds to reports, how reports are classified, and how serious concerns are escalated under DepEd procedures.

Speakup Connect should support MONHS’s existing responsibilities. It should not replace the school’s authority, DepEd procedures, guidance personnel, or child protection process.

# Bottom Line

The principal’s concern about the Privacy Act and Child Protection Policy is valid and should be treated as a strength of the implementation plan.

It means MONHS is thinking responsibly.

Speakup Connect can still move forward, but it should move forward as a controlled, privacy-aware, child-protection-aware pilot with MONHS defining the official governance and the app configured to support those rules.

The app should support MONHS’s existing responsibilities. It should not replace the school’s authority, DepEd procedures, guidance personnel, or child protection process.
